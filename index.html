A Quarter at UCLA

Introduction

Well, starting off, I definitely did not prepare early enough. Most of what I had to do was done roughly a month or two before the start of my quarter at UCLA.

To take a step back:
At the NAK we all have the not entirely normal, yet typical, half-year semester. This is not the case at UCLA or in most universities in the States, for that matter.
Here, mostly everything is based on the quarterly system (which, as a side note, is a subjectively preferable choice).

Academic Highlights

Generally, at UCLA, you have a lot of choices from the UCLA Extension program.
They are pricey, though, so here is what I chose:

  • Principles of Macroeconomics : $722
  • Global Supply Chain Management : $855
  • Cost Accounting & Analysis : $855
  • HTML & CSS : $1095

I chose the first three based on subjects/classes that are mandatory in the Business-IT curriculum of the NAK.
HTML & CSS is one of the 3 mandatory free-choices, which in turns mean 2 more choices are waiting for me in Germany.

Learning Agreements

I completed the learning agreement way too late—about a month before my flight. But the NAK employees came through and checked my learning agreements within days, ensuring I was all set for my UCLA courses.
Don't forget that these are mandatory to be able to get the grades of the chosen abroad classes acknowledged.

Exploring Los Angeles

Los Angeles is a city that offers something for everyone—from iconic landmarks to hidden gems. My time exploring LA was packed with adventures, but the real highlight was sharing those experiences with others.

Santa Monica & Venice Beach

Santa Monica Pier is as iconic as you'd imagine, and Venice Beach has its own unique vibe. Whether you're walking along the boardwalk, checking out street performers, or just soaking up the ocean views, these spots are a must-see.

Marina del Rey

A quieter alternative to Santa Monica and Venice, Marina del Rey is perfect for relaxing by the water or grabbing a bite at one of the many waterfront restaurants.

Getty Museum & Griffith Observatory

The Getty Museum offers incredible art and even better views, while the Griffith Observatory is great for a night out with breathtaking cityscapes and stargazing.

Hollywood & Beverly Hills

The Walk of Fame, Beverly Hills, and other tourist hotspots are worth visiting for the photos and the experience. Just don't set your expectations too high—it can get crowded.

Downtown LA, K-Town, & Arts District

Downtown has its charm, but places like K-Town and the Arts District have their own energy. You’ll find amazing food and cool spots for a night out.

Compton (Drive Through)

Compton is an area I passed through—it’s probably not a spot I’d recommend exploring on foot, but driving through gives a different perspective of LA.

Universal Studios

Universal Studios is a fun experience, but if you're deciding between this and Disney, I’d honestly recommend Disney based on my friend's feedback.

Key Tip

Exploring LA is as much about the people as it is about the places. Finding a group to go on adventures with makes the experience a hundred times better. And luckily, meeting people is surprisingly easy!

Reflection

Applying to UCLA was a straightforward process through Mobility Online and the UCLA Extension Portal. There were some fees involved, including an initial payment of $500 upon acceptance. The total payment for the quarter was around $3800, with the remainder ($3300) due a month before the start.

Worth mentioning: Only the $500 for acceptance is needed to get a visa.

This experience at UCLA has been transformative in so many ways, from adapting to a new academic environment to exploring a vibrant city like Los Angeles. I’ve gained a broader perspective on my studies and personal growth, and would encourage anyone interested in an exchange program to embrace the opportunity fully.
