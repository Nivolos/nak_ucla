Exploring Los Angeles

Los Angeles is a city that offers something for everyone‚Äîfrom iconic landmarks to hidden gems. My time exploring LA was packed with adventures, but the real highlight was sharing those experiences with others.

Santa Monica & Venice Beach

Santa Monica Pier is as iconic as you'd imagine, and Venice Beach has its own unique vibe. Whether you're walking along the boardwalk, checking out street performers, or just soaking up the ocean views, these beaches are worth hanging out and about on. Also Santa Monica Pier.

Marina del Rey

A quieter alternative to Santa Monica and Venice, Marina del Rey is perfect for relaxing by the water or grabbing a bite at one of the many waterfront restaurants. Also while youre at UCLA you will get the chance to go kayaking with other extension students - would most definitely recommend!

Getty Museum & Griffith Observatory

The Getty Museum offers does offer art but to be honest I'm all about the view of the city, probably one of the best views of LA. The Griffith Observatory is great for hiking towards the Hollywood Sign or a night out with breathtaking cityscapes and stargazing. It also has an amazing view of LA, especially at nighttime.

Hollywood & Beverly Hills

The Walk of Fame, Beverly Hills, and other tourist hotspots are worth visiting for the photos and the experience. Just don't set your expectations too high‚Äîit can get crowded. Oh and the Walk of Fame is rather dirty and camping grounds for homeless people.

Downtown LA, K-Town, & Arts District

Downtown has its charm, but places like KoreaTown and the ArtsDistrict have their own energy. You‚Äôll find amazing food and cool spots for a night out. KTown never sleeps and has some amazing food (if you like asian foods)

Compton (Drive Through)

Compton is an area I passed through‚Äîit‚Äôs probably not an area I‚Äôd recommend exploring on foot, but driving through gives a different perspective of LA. Its a bit like the movies depict - cars jacked up on cinder blocks without any wheels, others taken apart partially. It really doesnt feel safe.

Universal Studios

Universal Studios is a fun experience, but if you're deciding between this and Disney, I‚Äôd honestly recommend Disney based on my friend's feedback. Thing is the price ($100+ for Universal, $200+ for Disney for a Day Ticket)

Malibu

Malibu offers stunning beaches and a more laid-back vibe compared to the busier spots in LA. It‚Äôs perfect for a relaxing day trip, with scenic drives and great seafood restaurants along the coast. Or, as of recent news, home to wildfires that diminish everything in their paths

Key Tip

Exploring LA is as much about the people as it is about the places. Finding a group to go on adventures with makes the experience a hundred times better. And luckily, meeting people is super easy, since everybody is coming to LA from all over the World and are willing and looking to connect.

Reflection

Applying to UCLA was a straightforward process through Mobility Online and the UCLA Extension Portal. There were some fees involved, including an initial payment of $500 upon acceptance. The total payment for the quarter was around $3800, with the remainder ($3300) due a month before the start.

Worth mentioning: Only the $500 for acceptance is needed to get a visa.

This experience at UCLA has been transformative in so many ways, from adapting to a new academic environment to exploring a vibrant city like Los Angeles. I‚Äôve gained a broader perspective on my studies and personal growth, and would encourage anyone interested in an exchange program to embrace the opportunity fully.
